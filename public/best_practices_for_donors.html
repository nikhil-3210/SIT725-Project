Best Practices for Donors

Helping those in need starts with **safe and responsible food donation**. Follow these best practices to ensure your contributions make a meaningful impact.

1. Food Safety Guidelines

  • Ensure **food is fresh** and not expired.
  • Store perishable items at **safe temperatures**.
  • Avoid donating food that has **been opened or partially used**.
  • Use **clean containers** and proper storage methods.

2. Proper Packaging & Labeling

  • Use **airtight and food-grade containers**.
  • Label items with **clear expiration dates and ingredients**.
  • Specify if food contains **allergens like nuts, dairy, or gluten**.

3. Ethical & Sustainable Practices

  • Donate food in **moderate portions** to avoid waste.
  • If possible, **avoid plastic** and use biodegradable packaging.
  • Support **local food banks** and community kitchens.

4. Be Aware of Legal & Health Guidelines

Many countries have **Good Samaritan Food Donation laws** that protect donors from liability. Always follow **local health regulations** and **partner with verified organizations** to distribute food safely.
